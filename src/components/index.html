Rise of Social Commerce in Uganda

1. Social commerce in Uganda has evolved rapidly over the past years,driven by the following;

    • Through high mobile phone penetration in Uganda. • Growing internet access in Uganda. • Affordability of social commerce in Uganda. • Convenience of social commerce in Uganda. • Increasing demand for products and services on social media platforms. • The rise of ecommerce in Africa. • Ugandan government’s support towards digital economy development.

The key milstones in development of social commerce in Uganda include; 2015 Facebook launched the ‘marketplace’ feature in Uganda,allowing users to buy and sell goods and services within the platform. 2016 twitter launched the ‘shop module’ feature in Uganda, allowing businesses to showcase their products and services directly within tweets. 2017 Whatsapp launched the ‘Business App’ in Uganda, providing businesses with tools to communicate with customers and sell products and services within the platform. 2018 Instagram launched the ‘shopping’ feature in Uganda, allowing businesses to tag products in their posts and stories so that users can easily learn more and purchase them. 2019 The Ugandan government launched the ‘ digital Uganda’ initiative, aimed at promoting the adoption and use of digital technologies to drive economic growth and development. Social commerce examples in Uganda include Jumia for shopping products and buying of food, Glove for shopping food and other products, kikuubo online, safe boda app ,faras app for transportation etc
